Malaria Cell Detection

Introduction

  • Malaria is a life threatening disease caused by parasites that are trasmitted in people through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

  • It remains a major burden on global health, with roughly 200 million cases worldwide and 4 hundred thousand deaths per year.

  • The standard way of diagnoising malaria is by visually examining blood smears for parasite-infected red blood cells under the microscope by qualified technicians.

  • This method is inefficient on the large scale and the diagnosis depends upon the experience and the knowledge of the person doing the examination.

Malaria Cell Detector


Knowing the disadvantage of the standard way of diagnosis of Malaria, I made a web based application that uses Deep learning (Convolutional Neural Network) to classify the microscopic images to tell whether it is infected or not.
This application is based on a deep learning model that has been trained on thousands of similar microscopic images and has the calculated accuracy of 91-94 %.
